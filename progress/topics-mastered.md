### Insurance Planning and Risk Management

#### Homeowners Insurance Special Limits (2025-10-13) - Medium-High
- HO policy types (HO-2 named perils, HO-3 open perils, HO-4 renters, HO-6 condo)
- Coverage A/B/C/D structure (dwelling, other structures, personal property, loss of use)
- **Special limits**: Jewelry/furs $1,500, coins $200, firearms/silver/business $2,500
- Property away from home: 10% of Coverage C
- 80% coinsurance rule understanding

#### Auto Insurance PAP Structure (2025-10-15) - High
- Part A (Liability): Split limits calculation (per person/per accident/property damage)
- Part B (Medical Payments): No deductible, coordinates with health insurance
- Part C (UM/UIM): Protection against uninsured/underinsured drivers
- Part D (Physical Damage): Collision vs comprehensive with deductibles
- Covered persons: Named insured, family, permissive users
- Rental car coverage: Liability follows insured

#### Social Security Filing Timeline (2025-10-13) - High
- Early (62-67): 5-7% reduction per year
- FRA (67): 100% benefit
- Delayed (67-70): 8% increase per year, no further increase after 70
- Break-even analysis around age 78-80

#### Social Security Earnings Test (2025-10-13) - High
- Applies only before FRA
- 2024 threshold: $22,320 (lose $1 for every $2 over)
- After FRA: Can earn unlimited with no penalty
- Understanding threshold application

#### Social Security Spousal Benefits (2025-10-13) - High
- Up to 50% of worker's FRA benefit
- Requirements: Spouse age 62+ AND worker must have filed
- Timeline implications: Earlier filing = earlier spousal benefits

#### Social Security Taxation (2025-10-13) - Medium-High
- Combined income = AGI + tax-exempt interest + 50% of SS
- Thresholds (married): <$32k = 0%, $32k-$44k = up to 50%, >$44k = up to 85%
- Same regardless of filing age

#### Retirement Plan Types (2025-10-13) - High
- 403(b): Non-profit/education employees
- 457(b): Government employees, same limits as 401k/403b, can stack with them
- 457(f): Executive golden handcuffs, unlimited deferral, substantial forfeiture risk
- 2024 limits: $23k base + $7.5k catch-up = $30.5k
- All employer plans limited by compensation

#### Keogh Plans (HR-10) (2025-10-13) - High
- For self-employed individuals
- Calculation: 20% of adjusted net self-employment income (after ½ SE tax deduction)
- Separate from IRA limits - can contribute to both
- Overall limit: $66k (2024), $69k (2025)

#### Medicare Parts A/B/C/D Structure (2025-10-15) - Medium-High
- Part A (Hospital): Free if 40+ quarters worked, covers hospital/SNF/hospice/home health
- Part B (Medical): Doctor visits, preventive care, labs, outpatient; $174.70/month + IRMAA
- Part C (Medicare Advantage): Private insurance replacing A+B, network restrictions
- Part D (Prescription Drugs): Standalone drug coverage, varies by plan
- Corrected common confusion: C = Advantage, D = Drugs (not reversed)

#### Medicare Enrollment and Penalties (2025-10-15) - Medium-High
- Initial Enrollment Period: 7-month window around 65th birthday
- Part B penalty: 10% per 12-month period delayed (lifetime penalty)
- Part D penalty: 1% per month delayed (lifetime penalty)
- Exception: Creditable employer coverage (20+ employees) allows delay without penalty
- Part A is free and can be enrolled separately from Part B

#### Medicare Cost-Sharing (2025-10-15) - Medium
- Hospital days 1-60: Deductible only ($1,632/2024)
- Hospital days 61-90: $408/day coinsurance
- Hospital days 91-150: Lifetime reserve ($816/day, 60 days total for life)
- SNF days 1-20: Free (after 3-day hospital stay)
- SNF days 21-100: $204/day coinsurance
- IRMAA: Income-based premium adjustment for high earners

### Investment Planning

#### R-squared and Risk Decomposition (2025-10-11) - High
- Formula: Unsystematic risk = 1 - R², where R² = correlation²
- R² represents systematic (market) risk percentage
- Can identify relevant vs irrelevant data in problems
- Understands correlation interpretation and diversification implications

#### Short Selling Mechanics (2025-10-11) - High
- Understands borrow-sell-buyback-return process
- Unlimited loss potential, no expiration
- Use for indefinite timeline bearish bets

#### Options Strategies (2025-10-11) - High
- Put options: Limited risk (premium only), has expiration
- Knows when to use puts vs short selling (timing confidence vs indefinite)
- Understands max loss on long options = premium only

#### Systematic vs Unsystematic Risk (2025-10-11) - High
- Market risk vs company-specific risk
- Diversification benefits
- Higher correlation = more systematic risk, less diversification benefit

### Tax Planning

#### Traditional IRA Contributions & Deductions (2025-10-11) - High
- Earned income requirement (wages/salary count; pension/SS/investments don't)
- Contribution limit: Lesser of earned income or $7k + $1k catch-up = $8k (2024)
- Contribute vs deduct distinction: Can contribute even if can't deduct
- Active participant phase-out: $77k-$87k (single, 2024)

#### Roth IRA Strategy (2025-10-11) - High
- Better than non-deductible traditional IRA (tax-free vs tax-deferred growth)

#### Qualified Charitable Distributions (QCD) (2025-10-11) - High
- Age 70½ eligibility
- Excludes from income (not just deduction) - keeps AGI low
- Can count toward RMD requirement
- Better than withdraw + donate for AGI management

#### Capital Gains/Losses (2025-10-11) - High
- **Netting**: Net ST, net LT, then combine; result keeps character of larger
- **Tax rates**: ST = ordinary rates; LT = 0%/15%/20% based on ordinary bracket
- Perfect execution of netting calculations

#### Tax Doctrines (2025-10-11) - High
- **Step Transaction**: IRS collapses pre-planned multiple steps into one
- **Constructive Receipt**: Income taxable when available
- **Assignment of Income**: Taxed to earner, can't avoid by assigning
- **Tax-Deferred Exchange**: 1031 like-kind exchanges

#### Passive Activity Losses (2025-10-11) - High
- Rental losses = passive; can only offset passive income
- $25k exception for active participants (phases out $100k-$150k AGI)
- **Disposition rule**: ALL suspended losses released when property sold
- Understands AGI phase-out thresholds

### Retirement Savings and Income Planning

#### Retirement Plan Classifications (2025-10-11) - High
- **DC vs DB**: Based on what's defined and who bears investment risk
  - DC: Contribution known, benefit unknown; employee bears risk
  - DB: Benefit promised, contribution varies; employer bears risk
- **Pension vs Profit-Sharing**: Mandatory vs discretionary contributions
- **Tricky plans**: Cash Balance (DB despite account balance), Target Benefit (DC), Money Purchase Pension (DC with mandatory contributions)

#### Nondiscrimination Testing (2025-10-11) - High
- **HCE Definition**: 5% owner OR $155k+ compensation
- **Ratio Percentage Test**: (non-HCE %) ÷ (HCE %) ≥ 70%
- **Average Benefits Test**: (Avg non-HCE benefit) ÷ (Avg HCE benefit) ≥ 70%
- Can calculate correctly once formula is understood

### Estate Planning

#### Probate vs Non-Probate Assets (2025-10-11) - High
- **Goes through probate**: Solely-owned property, tenants in common, life insurance payable to estate
- **Avoids probate**: Beneficiary designations (to persons), JTWROS, trusts, TOD/POD accounts

#### Life Insurance Beneficiary Strategies (2025-10-11) - High
- To person → Avoids probate
- To estate → Goes through probate (strategic for estate liquidity or by mistake)
- Understands practical applications and estate liquidity planning

#### Property Ownership Types (2025-10-11) - High
- **JTWROS**: Passes automatically to surviving owner, avoids probate
- **Tenants in Common**: Share goes through probate, no automatic transfer
